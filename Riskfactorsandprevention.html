Risk Factors

There is no single risk factor that provokes MS, but several factors are believed to contribute to the overall risk.

IMMUNOLOGIC FACTORS

  • Cytotoxic or “killer” T cells directly attack and destroy cells bearing certain characteristics
  • T regulatory cells, a type of T cell, dampen or turn off inflammation. In MS, T regulatory cells to not function correctly and do not effectively turn off inflammation.
ENVIRONMENTAL FACTORS

  • Geographic Gradient
  • Vitamin D
  • Smoking
  • Obesity
WHO IS MORE AT RISK FOR BREAST CANCER?
  • White people, particularly those of Northern European descent, are at highest risk of developing MS . People of Asian, African or Native American descent have the lowest risk

There have been many theories suggested and researched as possible causes of MS, but many do not have enough evidence to support whether they may be a cause MS.

Prevention Overview

It cannot be totally prevented, as there are some risk factors for the condition that you cannot change, such as your age and genetics. However, making some changes to your lifestyle can help reduce your risk of developing MS. For example, making sure that you get enough vitamin D in your diet and quitting smoking.
